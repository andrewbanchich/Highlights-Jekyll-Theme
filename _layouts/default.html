Who I am

I received my Phd in Linguistics from The University of Texas at Austin in 2020. At the UTsoundLab (PI: Dr. Rajka Smiljanic) since 2015, my research focuses on speech perception and production, first and second language processing and auditory memory. Two peer-reviewed articles were published from my dissertation ("Memory for speech of varying intelligibility: effects of perception and production of clear speech on recall and recognition memory for native and non-native listeners and talkers") #1 and #2. You can watch a narrated recording of my dissertation slides on YouTube. I use GitHub/this page to host some of my research output.
For a full list of publications and activities, see my CV. Outside the lab, I love science-fiction, singing in noraebang, and doing crochet.
